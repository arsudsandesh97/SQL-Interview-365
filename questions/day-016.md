# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Customer Subscription Reactivation Analysis

## Business Scenario
You are a Data Analyst at a growing E-commerce SaaS company that offers monthly subscription boxes. The marketing team is keen on understanding customer churn and reactivation patterns to better target dormant customers and assess the success of win-back campaigns. They need to identify customers who have reactivated their subscriptions after previously cancelling, quantify the duration they were inactive (dormancy period), and measure how long their new active subscription lasts. This information will help refine their marketing strategies and improve customer lifetime value.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'
```

---

# Database Setup

## Create customer_subscription_history Table
```sql
CREATE TABLE customer_subscription_history (
    subscription_id VARCHAR(50) PRIMARY KEY,
    customer_id VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    start_date DATE NOT NULL,
    end_date DATE NULL -- NULL if the subscription is currently active
);
```

## Insert customer_subscription_history Data
```sql
INSERT INTO customer_subscription_history (subscription_id, customer_id, start_date, end_date) VALUES
('SUB001', 'CUST001', '2022-01-01', '2022-06-30'), -- Canceled
('SUB002', 'CUST001', '2022-09-01', NULL),        -- Reactivated, currently active
('SUB003', 'CUST002', '2022-03-15', NULL),        -- Active, never canceled
('SUB004', 'CUST003', '2022-04-01', '2022-12-31'), -- Canceled, never reactivated
('SUB005', 'CUST004', '2021-05-01', '2021-12-31'), -- Canceled
('SUB006', 'CUST004', '2022-03-01', '2022-08-31'), -- Reactivated, then canceled again
('SUB007', 'CUST004', '2022-10-15', NULL),        -- Reactivated again, currently active
('SUB008', 'CUST005', '2023-01-01', '2023-03-31'), -- Canceled
('SUB009', 'CUST005', '2023-04-15', '2023-08-15'); -- Reactivated, then canceled again
```

# Task
Identify all instances where a customer has reactivated their subscription. For each reactivation, calculate:
1.  The `dormancy_period_days`: The number of days between the `end_date` of their *previous* subscription and the `start_date` of their *new* (reactivated) subscription.
2.  The `new_active_duration_days`: The total number of days the reactivated subscription was/is active. If the `end_date` for the reactivated subscription is NULL, use `CURRENT_DATE` to calculate its duration.

Exclude any subscriptions that have no prior `end_date` (i.e., they are a customer's first subscription or their previous subscription is still active).

Order the results by `customer_id` and then `reactivation_start_date`.
