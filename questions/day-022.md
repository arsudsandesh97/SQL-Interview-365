# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: E-commerce Customer Engagement Decay Identification

## Business Scenario
An E-commerce company is experiencing fluctuations in customer engagement and wants to proactively identify customers who are showing signs of "decay" in their purchasing behavior. As a Data Analyst, your task is to flag these at-risk customers so the marketing team can implement targeted re-engagement campaigns. The definition of engagement decay for this analysis is a significant drop in average weekly order count over a specific recent period compared to a prior period.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-11-15'
```

# Database Setup

## Create customers Table
```sql
CREATE TABLE customers (
    customer_id INT PRIMARY KEY,
    customer_name VARCHAR(50),
    registration_date DATE
);
```

# Task
Identify customers who exhibit "engagement decay" based on their order frequency. A customer is considered to have engagement decay if:
1.  Their average weekly order count in the **last 28 days** (Recent Period: `CURRENT_DATE - 28 days` to `CURRENT_DATE - 1 day`) has decreased by **more than 25%** compared to their average weekly order count in the **28 days immediately prior** (Previous Period: `CURRENT_DATE - 56 days` to `CURRENT_DATE - 29 days`).
2.  Additionally, their average weekly order count in the Recent Period must be **less than 1.0**. This filters out customers who already had very low activity in the prior period and whose "drop" might not be truly significant.

Your query should return `customer_id`, `customer_name`, `recent_avg_weekly_orders`, `previous_avg_weekly_orders`, and `percentage_drop`.
Handle cases where `previous_avg_weekly_orders` is zero gracefully (e.g., if a customer ordered for the first time in the recent period, they shouldn't be flagged for a "drop").
