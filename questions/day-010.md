# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Post-Return Category Shift Analysis

## Business Scenario
An e-commerce company's Marketing and Product teams are concerned about customer retention and loyalty after a product return. They want to understand if returning an item from a specific product category influences a customer's subsequent purchasing behavior, specifically whether they shift their primary purchase interest away from the returned category. As a Data Analyst, you've been asked to identify customers who exhibit this specific behavior to help inform targeted marketing campaigns or product recommendations.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'
```

# Task
Identify customers who made a purchase, then returned an item from a specific product category, and subsequently made *another purchase* where the *most frequently purchased product category* in their **first order placed after that return** is *different* from the category of the returned item.

For customers who satisfy these conditions, return the `customer_id`, `customer_name`, the `category` of the item they returned, the `order_id` and `order_date` of their first purchase *after* the return, and the `primary_category_in_post_return_order` (defined as the category with the highest total quantity of items in that specific post-return order. In case of a tie in total quantity across categories, any one of the tied categories is acceptable).
