# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Estimating Lost Sales Due to Stockouts

## Business Scenario
A large electronics retailer is struggling with inventory management, leading to frequent stockouts for popular products. The operations team wants to quantify the impact of these stockouts by estimating "lost sales". Your task as a Data Analyst is to identify products that experienced stockouts and calculate the total estimated units they *could have sold* during those stockout periods. This estimation should be based on each product's average daily sales *only on days when it was in stock and had positive sales*. The analysis should cover the period from '2023-12-01' up to and including the `CURRENT_DATE`.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-12-07'
```

# Task
Calculate the `estimated_lost_sales_units` for each product due to stockouts in the period from '2023-12-01' up to and including the `CURRENT_DATE`.
The estimation method is as follows:
1.  Determine the `average_daily_sales` for each product. This average should only consider days where the product was *in stock* (`units_available > 0`) AND had *positive sales* (`units_sold > 0`). If a product never had sales while in stock, its average daily sales should be considered 0.
2.  Identify all distinct days within the analysis period where the product was *out of stock* (`units_available = 0`). Count these as `stockout_days`.
3.  `estimated_lost_sales_units` = `stockout_days` * `average_daily_sales`.
4.  Only return products that have a non-zero `estimated_lost_sales_units`, ordered by `product_id`.
