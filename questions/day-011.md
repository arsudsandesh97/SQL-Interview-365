# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Identify SaaS Users at Risk of Churn Due to Activity Decline

## Business Scenario
You are a Data Analyst at a growing SaaS company that offers a project management platform. User retention is a critical metric, and the company wants to proactively identify users who might be at risk of churning. Your Operations team has observed that a significant drop in a user's weekly active days often precedes churn. They define "at-risk" as a user whose active days in the most recent full week have dropped by more than 25% compared to their average weekly active days over the preceding four full weeks. You need to provide a list of such users.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-23'
```

# Task
Given `CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-23'`, identify all `user_id`s who are "at-risk" of churning.
A user is "at-risk" if their distinct active days in the Most Recent Full Week (MRFW) have dropped by more than 25% compared to their average distinct active days per week in the Preceding Four Full Weeks (P4FW).

Definitions:
- **Active Day**: Any day a `user_id` has at least one entry in `user_activity_logs`.
- **CURRENT_DATE**: '2023-10-23' (a Monday).
- **Most Recent Full Week (MRFW)**: The 7-day period ending on the Saturday immediately prior to `CURRENT_DATE`. For '2023-10-23', this is '2023-10-15' (Sunday) to '2023-10-21' (Saturday).
- **Preceding Four Full Weeks (P4FW)**: The 28-day period (4 full weeks) immediately preceding the MRFW. For `CURRENT_DATE`, this is '2023-09-17' (Sunday) to '2023-10-14' (Saturday).
- **At-risk condition**: `(MRFW_active_days < average_P4FW_weekly_active_days * 0.75)`
- Users without enough historical data (e.g., no activity in P4FW or MRFW) should be excluded from this specific 'at-risk' analysis, unless they meet the criteria based on available data. For this problem, focus on users with data in both periods.

The output should include the `user_id`, their active days in the MRFW, their average active days per week in the P4FW, and a `status` indicating if they are 'At Risk' or 'Not At Risk'. Sort the results by `user_id`.
