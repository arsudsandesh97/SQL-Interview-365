# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: E-commerce Customer Return Behavior Analysis

## Business Scenario
An E-commerce company is experiencing high return rates and wants to identify customers who exhibit "problematic return behavior" but are still valuable. As a Data Analyst, you've been tasked with pinpointing these specific customers. The marketing and operations teams want to reach out to these customers to understand their feedback, potentially offer incentives, or identify product issues.

A customer is considered to have "problematic return behavior" if, within the last 6 months, they meet *all* of the following criteria:
1.  They have placed at least 3 distinct orders.
2.  They have purchased a total of at least 5 individual items across all their orders.
3.  Their "Adjusted Return Rate" (ARR) is greater than 40%. The ARR is calculated as:
    (Total quantity of returned items *not* due to 'Damaged in transit' or 'Wrong item shipped') / (Total quantity of all purchased items)

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-12-31'
```

```sql

    customer_id INT PRIMARY KEY,
    customer_name VARCHAR(100),
    email VARCHAR(100) UNIQUE
```

```sql
CREATE TABLE returns (
    return_id INT PRIMARY KEY,
```

# Task
Identify all customers who meet the "problematic return behavior" criteria within the last 6 months from `CURRENT_DATE`.
The analysis period should be `CURRENT_DATE - INTERVAL '6 MONTH'` to `CURRENT_DATE`.
Your output should include the `customer_id`, `customer_name`, `total_orders_last_6_months`, `total_items_purchased_last_6_months`, and `adjusted_return_rate`. The `adjusted_return_rate` should be a decimal value.
