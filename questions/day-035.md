# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: E-commerce Subscriber Churn and Reactivation Analysis

## Business Scenario
You are a Data Analyst at 'SubscribrX', an e-commerce platform offering premium subscription boxes. The marketing and customer retention teams want to deeply understand customer churn and reactivation patterns. They need to identify customers who have cancelled their subscriptions but later decided to resubscribe. For these "win-back" customers, they are particularly interested in how long it typically takes them to reactivate. Additionally, they want a clear picture of the current status of all customers in the system, distinguishing between those who are currently active and those who have churned, while also highlighting their reactivation history.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'
```

# Task
Write a SQL query that returns the following for each customer:
1. `customer_id`
2. `current_status`: 'Active' if their latest subscription is ongoing (end_date IS NULL), otherwise 'Churned'.
3. `total_reactivations`: The total count of times a customer has reactivated their subscription. A reactivation occurs when a `start_date` of a new subscription is strictly after the `end_date` of a previous subscription for the same customer. If no reactivations, this should be 0.
4. `min_days_to_reactivate`: The minimum number of days it took for the customer to reactivate across all their reactivation events. This is calculated as `start_date` of the new subscription minus `end_date` of the previous subscription. If no reactivations, this should be NULL.

Order the results by `customer_id`.
