# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Identify Stale Inventory for Capital Optimization

## Business Scenario
A leading E-commerce company, "SwiftShip," is concerned about capital tied up in slow-moving or "stale" inventory. As a Data Analyst, your task is to identify products that meet the criteria for stale inventory so that the operations team can devise strategies for clearance, such as promotions or discounts, to free up warehouse space and capital. This requires a precise definition of "stale" inventory, which includes products that are currently active and in stock but haven't had recent sales, while also ensuring they *have* sold in the past (to exclude new products that just haven't had time to sell yet).

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-11-15'
```

# Task
Write a SQL query to identify "stale" inventory based on the following criteria:
1.  The product `status` must be 'Active'.
2.  The `stock_level` must be greater than 0.
3.  The product must not have had any sales in the last 90 days from `CURRENT_DATE`.
4.  The product *must* have historical sales (i.e., it has sold at least once *before* the 90-day lookback period, or at any point in the past). This condition differentiates genuinely stale products from brand new items that simply haven't sold yet.

Your query should return `product_id`, `product_name`, `stock_level`, and `last_sale_date`. If a product has had historical sales but no sales within the last 90 days, `last_sale_date` should reflect the actual last sale date.
