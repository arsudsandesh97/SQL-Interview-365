# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Identifying At-Risk Customers Based on Spending Decline

## Business Scenario
You are a Data Analyst for "Trendify," an e-commerce company. The marketing and customer success teams are concerned about customer churn. They want to proactively identify customers who are showing signs of disengagement by significantly reducing their spending. Your task is to develop a SQL query to pinpoint "at-risk" customers based on a specific behavioral pattern.

An "at-risk" customer is defined as someone whose average monthly purchase value in the last three full calendar months has dropped by more than 25% compared to their average monthly purchase value in the preceding six full calendar months. Only customers who had active purchases in the earlier historical period should be considered for this analysis.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-11-15'
```

# Task
Write a SQL query to identify "at-risk" customers.
A customer is "at-risk" if:
1.  They had at least one purchase in the **Historical Period**.
    *   The Historical Period consists of the 6 full calendar months immediately preceding the last 3 full calendar months before `CURRENT_DATE`.
    *   For `CURRENT_DATE = '2023-11-15'`, this means February 2023 to July 2023 (inclusive).
2.  Their average monthly purchase value in the **Recent Period** is less than 75% of their average monthly purchase value in the **Historical Period**.
    *   The Recent Period consists of the last 3 full calendar months immediately preceding `CURRENT_DATE`.
    *   For `CURRENT_DATE = '2023-11-15'`, this means August 2023 to October 2023 (inclusive).
    *   If a customer had no purchases in the Recent Period but did in the Historical Period, their `recent_avg_monthly_spend` should be considered `0`.

Your query should return the `customer_id`, their calculated `historical_avg_monthly_spend`, and their calculated `recent_avg_monthly_spend`, formatted to two decimal places. Sort the results by `customer_id` in ascending order.
