# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Identifying Worsening Delivery Performance by Carrier and Region

## Business Scenario
SwiftShip Inc., an e-commerce company, prioritizes timely deliveries for customer satisfaction. The Operations team has observed an uptick in customer complaints regarding late deliveries and suspects certain shipping partners or delivery regions might be underperforming. As a Data Analyst, your task is to identify specific shipping carriers and delivery regions that have shown a *recent significant deterioration* in delivery performance. This involves comparing their average delivery delay in the last 30 days to their average delay in the preceding 60 days. The Operations team needs to flag combinations where recent performance is not only worse but also exceeds a company-wide unacceptable average delay threshold of 120 minutes (2 hours). This analysis will enable SwiftShip Inc. to prioritize investigations and address problematic areas effectively.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'
```

# Task
Write a SQL query that identifies carrier-region combinations exhibiting a "significant deterioration" in delivery performance.
A combination is considered to have "significant deterioration" if **both** of the following conditions are met:
1.  The average delivery delay (in minutes) for orders delivered in the *last 30 days* (up to `CURRENT_DATE`) is strictly greater than their average delivery delay for orders delivered in the *preceding 60 days* (the 60 days immediately before the last 30 days).
2.  The average delivery delay in the *last 30 days* for that carrier-region combination exceeds 120 minutes (2 hours).

Calculate delays as `GREATEST(0, DATEDIFF(minute, expected_delivery_date, delivery_date))`. Only consider orders that have a `delivery_date` and where `expected_delivery_date` is not NULL.

Your output should include the `carrier_name`, `delivery_region`, `current_period_avg_delay_minutes`, and `historical_period_avg_delay_minutes`, ordered by `current_period_avg_delay_minutes` in descending order.
