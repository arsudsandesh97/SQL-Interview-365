# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Promotion Impact Analysis on Daily Sales Quantity

## Business Scenario
ElectroMart, an online electronics retailer, recently ran a 7-day "Spring Sale" promotion specifically on products within their 'Electronics' category. As a Data Analyst, your task is to evaluate the true impact of this promotion. The marketing team wants to understand if the promotion genuinely increased sales for these products or if it merely shifted purchases from other periods, potentially leading to a post-promotion slump. You need to compare the *average daily sales quantity* for promoted products during the 7-day promotion period, against the *average daily sales quantity* for the same products in the 7 days immediately *preceding* the promotion, and the 7 days immediately *following* the promotion.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'
```

---

# Database Setup

## Create products Table
```sql
CREATE TABLE products (
    product_id INT PRIMARY KEY,
    product_name VARCHAR(255),
    category VARCHAR(100)
);
```

# Task
Write a SQL query that identifies all products within the 'Electronics' category (the promoted category) and calculates their average daily sales quantity for three distinct 7-day periods:
1.  **Pre-promotion:** 7 days immediately preceding the promotion (August 25, 2023 - August 31, 2023).
2.  **During Promotion:** The 7-day promotion period (September 1, 2023 - September 7, 2023).
3.  **Post-promotion:** 7 days immediately following the promotion (September 8, 2023 - September 14, 2023).

If a product had no sales in a specific period, its average daily quantity for that period should be `0.00`. The average daily quantity should be rounded to two decimal places.
Order the results by `product_id`.

---

# Skills Tested
*   Date and Time Functions (e.g., `BETWEEN`, `DATE_SUB`, `DATE_ADD`)
*   Conditional Aggregation (`CASE WHEN` with `SUM`, `COUNT`)
*   Common Table Expressions (CTEs) or Subqueries for modularity
*   `LEFT JOIN` to ensure all promoted products are included, even with zero sales in a period
*   Aggregation (`SUM`, `AVG`)
*   Handling `NULL` values (`COALESCE` or `IFNULL`)
*   Rounding numeric results (`ROUND`)
