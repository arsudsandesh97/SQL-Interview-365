# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Identifying Re-Engaged Customers

## Business Scenario
A rapidly growing e-commerce company, "TrendSetter Fashions," offers a wide range of products. They are keen on understanding customer loyalty and re-engagement patterns. Specifically, the marketing team wants to identify customers who have shown a significant period of inactivity (a "churn risk" period) but then made a subsequent purchase, indicating a successful re-engagement. This insight will help them tailor re-engagement campaigns and understand customer lifecycle better. As a Data Analyst, you need to provide a list of such re-engaged customers and the date of their first order after re-engaging.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'
```

# Task
Write a SQL query to identify all customers who have "re-engaged" by making a purchase after a period of inactivity. A customer is considered to have re-engaged if there is a gap of more than 90 days between two consecutive orders (for that specific customer), and they then made the later order. For each re-engagement instance, return the `customer_id` and the `order_date` of the *first* order that marked the re-engagement (i.e., the order immediately following the 90+ day gap). Sort the results by `customer_id` and then `re_engagement_order_date`.
