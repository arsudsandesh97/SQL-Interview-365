# SQL Scenario-Based Interview Question: Identifying SaaS Churn-Risk Customers

## Business Scenario
A growing SaaS company, "InnovateSphere," is focused on proactive customer retention. Their Customer Success team needs to identify customers who are showing early signs of disengagement, specifically a significant drop in feature usage. You, as a Data Analyst, are tasked with providing a list of customers identified as "High Churn Risk" based on a recent usage decline. This list will help the Customer Success team prioritize their outreach and intervention efforts.

Assume:
```sql
CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'
```

# Task
Identify all customers who are currently at "High Churn Risk". A customer is considered "High Churn Risk" if their total `usage_count` across all features in the *most recent complete 30-day period* has dropped by more than 40% compared to their total `usage_count` in the *immediately preceding complete 30-day period*.

Define the periods relative to `CURRENT_DATE = '2023-10-26'`:
*   **Recent 30-day period (P2):** `2023-09-26` to `2023-10-25` (inclusive)
*   **Preceding 30-day period (P1):** `2023-08-27` to `2023-09-25` (inclusive)

Only consider customers who have *some usage* (i.e., `total_usage_p1` is not NULL and `total_usage_p2` is not NULL) in both periods (P1 and P2) to calculate a meaningful percentage drop.
Output the `customer_id`, `customer_name`, `total_usage_p1` (total usage in P1), `total_usage_p2` (total usage in P2), and `usage_drop_percentage` for these "High Churn Risk" customers. Sort the results by `customer_id`. The `usage_drop_percentage` should be rounded to 2 decimal places.
